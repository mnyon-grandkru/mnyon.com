I found Circle In the Round during college; it was a trusty cassette that got a lot of burn during study times. At this time, I'd heard Kind of Blue; I hadn't yet heard Bitches Brew. I think it would have been a worthy addition, but I don't have a time machine so I couldn't have made that possible.

If nothing else, this languid, ethereal musician All-Star Jam (we had Miles, Herbie Hancock, and Chick Corea amongst others on this track written by David Crosby) provided a vibe that is screwed without the promethazine, but not chopped. A fluid soundscape treat for the ears. It was great to relax, and ... one other thing.

In the 90s when I found this track, I was primarily an underground hip-hop head who HATED R&B. It go no run in my stereo, and I'd sometimes pull it out of other people's tape decks (ah, misguided youth). So if someone was coming over for quality time, I sure as hell wasn't playing Jodeci.
